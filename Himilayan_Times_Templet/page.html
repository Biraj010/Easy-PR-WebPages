Bishwakarma Puja, Vastu Day being observed across the country

Published: 10:02 am Sep 17, 2025

People are seen engaging at worship materials shop for Bishwakarma Puja in Manamaiju, Kathmandu.

KATHMANDU, SEPTEMBER 17

Metalworkers and artisans across the country are celebrating Bishwakarma Puja and Vastu Day today by worshipping tools and machinery and honoring Lord Bishwakarma, regarded as the divine architect.

Traditionally marked on Kanya Sankranti, the festival includes rituals in workshops and factories, where temporary clay or sand idols of Bishwakarma are made and later immersed in rivers.

Devotees believe that worshipping their equipment ensures durability and success in their craft.

Scholars say the tradition commemorates Bishwakarma’s legendary construction of the mythical city of Dwarka for Lord Krishna and his authorship of the ancient Vastu Shastra texts.

Nepal to Roll Out Electric Buses in Kathmandu

The government announced a new initiative to replace old diesel-run buses with modern electric buses across the Kathmandu Valley. Officials stated that this move is part of the broader goal to reduce air pollution and carbon emissions by 30% in the next five years.

According to the Ministry of Transport, the first fleet of 200 electric buses will be imported by mid-2026, with subsidies for local operators. Experts believe this initiative could significantly improve the Valley's deteriorating air quality.

Himalayan Glaciers Retreating at Alarming Rate

Recent studies warn that glaciers in the Himalayan region are melting faster than previously estimated. Researchers attribute this to rising global temperatures and unsustainable development in fragile mountain ecosystems.

The report highlights that if current trends continue, Nepal may face severe water shortages, threatening agriculture and hydropower production. Climate activists are urging policymakers to adopt stronger measures.

Dashain Travel Costs Surge Amid High Demand

Ticket prices for domestic flights and long-route buses have increased sharply ahead of the Dashain festival. Travelers have reported fares being almost double compared to regular days.

Authorities have instructed transport companies not to overcharge, but monitoring remains weak. Many citizens are concerned that the rising cost of travel will prevent families from celebrating together.

Tourism Revival in Pokhara as Foreign Visitors Return

Hotels and restaurants in Pokhara are witnessing a steady increase in bookings, signaling a revival in Nepal’s tourism industry. Adventure sports such as paragliding and trekking are gaining popularity among international travelers.

The Nepal Tourism Board has launched promotional campaigns in Europe and Asia to attract more tourists ahead of the peak season. Local entrepreneurs are optimistic that tourism revenue will surpass pre-pandemic levels within two years.

New Hydropower Projects to Add 1,500MW by 2027

Nepal Electricity Authority announced the completion of multiple hydropower projects, which are expected to add significant capacity to the national grid. The projects will help reduce dependence on electricity imports from India.

Officials also emphasized that surplus energy could be exported to neighboring countries, turning Nepal into a regional energy hub in the near future.

Education Sector Embraces Digital Classrooms

Private and government schools are gradually shifting towards digital education platforms. With increased internet penetration, many students in rural areas can now access online learning materials.

Experts suggest that blended learning—combining traditional methods with digital tools—could transform the education system, making it more inclusive and effective.

Healthcare System Under Pressure Amid Dengue Outbreak

Hospitals in major cities are struggling to cope with a rising number of dengue patients. The Ministry of Health has issued warnings about mosquito breeding sites and urged citizens to remain cautious.

Medical experts are calling for urgent action to improve public health infrastructure, as seasonal outbreaks continue to challenge the healthcare system every year.

Farmers Shift to Organic Agriculture in Terai

Farmers across the Terai region are switching to organic farming practices as demand for chemical-free food products grows in domestic and international markets.

The government is offering incentives, including subsidies on organic fertilizers, to encourage more farmers to adopt sustainable methods.

Nepal National Football Team Prepares for World Cup Qualifiers

The national football team has begun intensive training ahead of the FIFA World Cup qualifiers. Coaches are optimistic about the team’s performance, citing improvements in both physical fitness and tactical strategy.

Supporters across the country are rallying behind the team, with several fan clubs organizing events to boost morale.

Traditional Festivals Revive Cultural Heritage

Communities across Nepal are actively preserving their cultural identity by celebrating traditional festivals with renewed enthusiasm. Rituals, dances, and local cuisines are attracting tourists as well.

Cultural experts emphasize that festivals play a vital role in strengthening social bonds and ensuring the continuity of Nepal’s diverse heritage.
